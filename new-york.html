Hight line

The High Line is a 1.45-mile-long (2.33 km) elevated linear park,
greenway, and rail trail created on a former New York Central Railroad spur on the west side of Manhattan in New York City.
The High Line's design is a collaboration between James Corner Field Operations, Diller Scofidio + Renfro, and Piet Oudolf.
The abandoned spur has been redesigned as a "living system" drawing from multiple disciplines which include landscape architecture, urban design, and ecology.
The High Line was inspired by the 4.7 km (2.9 mi) long Coulée verte (tree-lined walkway), another elevated park in Paris completed in 1993.
